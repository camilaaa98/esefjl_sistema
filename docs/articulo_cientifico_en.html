Digital Transformation and Pharmaceutical Management at ESE Fabio Jaramillo Londoño in Caquetá

An Impact Analysis on Medical Supply Sovereignty and Patient Safety

Authors: Camila Guevara | ESEFJL Research Group

ABSTRACT

This article analyzes the implementation of the Farmacia ESEFJL system as a technological response to the challenges of the hospital network in the Caquetá department. The research demonstrates how the transition from manual records to a real-time management architecture reduces administrative risk and improves clinical safety through dynamic traffic light protocols. We conclude that pharmaceutical digitalization is a fundamental pillar for the sustainability of ESEs in difficult-to-access territories.

1. INTRODUCTION

The Fabio Jaramillo Londoño Social State Enterprise (ESE) operates in a geographically complex ecosystem, where the traceability of medical supplies is critical to guarantee the fundamental right to health. Historically, the lack of consolidated information has generated inefficiencies in stock replacement and risks in the expiration of high-rotation medications.
