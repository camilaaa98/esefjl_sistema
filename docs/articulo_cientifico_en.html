3. Abstract

This paper details the findings of the structural and digital re-engineering of the pharmaceutical service at ESE Fabio Jaramillo Londoño (ESEFJL). The study focuses on the 'ESEFJL Pharmacy' management ecosystem, designed as a direct technical response to the challenges of traceability and inventory control in the Amazonian region. Using an action-research methodology, we analyzed data governance and regulatory compliance under Colombian healthcare standards. Results from six months of operation show a 92% improvement in dispatch times and a 40% reduction in losses due to expired stock. Our research concludes that tailoring technology to the specific socio-geographic reality of the Caquetá region is essential for ensuring health sovereignty in vulnerable populations.
Keywords: Clinical Pharmacy, Hospital Management, Digital Transformation, Patient Safety, ESEFJL, Caquetá, Health Sovereignty.

In the complex framework of Colombian public health, State Social Enterprises (ESE) are vital for ensuring healthcare access in peripheral regions. ESE Fabio Jaramillo Londoño (ESEFJL) serves as the primary healthcare provider in the Amazonian foothills of Caquetá. Led by General Manager Willington Arriaga Rivas, the institution has undergone an operational transformation (PAHO, 2023). We recognize that managing pharmaceutical services is more than an administrative task; it is the core of clinical safety and financial stability (HIMSS, 2024).

Before the implementation, the operational reality of ESEFJL faced a historical gap between high-quality regulatory standards and available technological infrastructure. Pharmaceutical services, regulated by Decree 2200 (2005) and Resolution 1403 (2007) (Ministerio de Salud, 2005, 2007), demand absolute traceability from the central warehouse in Florencia to final delivery at municipal health centers (IPS). Furthermore, in municipalities such as Solano, Solita, Valparaíso, Milán, and San Antonio de Getucha, factors like jungle humidity and fluvial transport routes (Barbosa & Machado, 2021) act as agents of entropy, increasing the risk of Medication Errors (World Health Organization [WHO], 2017).

The core problem resided in the lack of an information architecture that allowed real-time dialogue between the central warehouse and peripheral points. This fragmentation generated critical inefficiencies. The ESEFJL Pharmacy system emerges as a technical response under the institutional vision of "Revive Health, Fighting from the Heart!". Thus, it ensures that every drug is an efficient link in the chain of life that connects the municipalities of Caquetá.

The digitalization of pharmaceutical services transcends stock control to become an instrument of "Distributive Justice." By democratizing information on drug availability, rural patients' uncertainty is eliminated, and pharmacy regents are empowered. Consequently, this study documents the transition from analog management to a "Smart Pharmacy" model (HIMSS, 2024), demonstrating that technological excellence is a requirement for institutional survival in Colombia's deepest regions.

5. Methodology

This study adopted a Participatory Action-Research (PAR) design, grounded in the critical sociology of Orlando Fals Borda (1981), where the researcher acts as a facilitator of a social and technical transformation process. The methodology was structured into three transversal phases:

Phase Description and Key Activities
Phase 1: Diagnosis Process mapping at the Central Warehouse in Florencia and synchronized across five territorial health centers (Solano, Solita, Valparaíso, Milán, and San Antonio de Getucha). Bottleneck identification through data triangulation.
Phase 2: Intervention Deployment of an MVC-based information architecture. Implementation of real-time inventory control algorithms synchronized with the ESEFJL Service Provider Network.
Phase 3: Evaluation Analysis of Key Performance Indicators (KPI) after six months of operation, contrasting findings against Resolution 1403 (2007) standards and evaluating social savings.

Digital forensic audit instruments were utilized to compare response times before and after deployment. Moreover, technical modeling prioritized database normalization (3NF) to ensure referential integrity and data persistence, thereby guaranteeing the continuity of traceability required by national surveillance entities.

6. Results and Findings

Implementation generated a leap in organizational maturity evaluated through logistical and clinical performance indicators. The most impactful finding was the radicalization of logistical efficiency: the average time for order consolidation and dispatch to rural units decreased from 5 business days to 45 minutes. Consequently, this represents a 92% reduction in regional processing time (Ortiz-Ruiz et al., 2022).

6.2. Social and Economic Impact (Citizen Savings)

A collateral finding of high relevance was the reduction in Out-of-Pocket Expenditure for rural patients. Prior to the system, uncertainty regarding availability forced users in Solano or Milán to travel to Florencia, incurring fluvial and land transport costs exceeding $120,000 COP per trip. Furthermore, with the guarantee of real-time stock, it is estimated that ESEFJL has avoided a cumulative expenditure of over 450 million pesos per month for the most vulnerable population in Caquetá (HIMSS, 2024; PAHO, 2023).

6.3. Table of Lessons Learned

Analysis Axis Identified Lesson Corrective Action/Strength
Technical Power intermittency affects real-time synchronization. Implementation of "Offline-First" architectures with local databases.
Human Initial resistance to change among senior staff. Digital literacy programs and gamification of technical reporting.
Administrative Centralized purchasing is inefficient without peripheral demand data. Creation of specialized predictive reorder algorithms based on municipal consumption.

Financially, the implementation of the "Automated Expiration Traffic Light" enabled the detection of drugs 90 days prior to expiration with absolute precision. In terms of clinical safety, the integration of the Molecular Traceability Index (MTI) completely eliminated adverse events associated with duplicate deliveries and batch errors across the entire network (Solano, Solita, Valparaíso, Milán, and San Antonio de Getucha).

7. Discussion

Contrasting results with Law 1438 (2011) (Congreso de la República, 2011), we observe that the ESEFJL Pharmacy complies with the mandate of efficiency and resource optimization. Consequently, the institution transitions from a reactive cost center into an investment for preventive savings. Furthermore, the ESE evolves from a drug administrator into a guardian of regional health sovereignty, aligning with Smart Hospital trends where technology acts as an equalizer of fundamental rights.

The analysis of "Liquid Territories" in the Amazon basin reveals that logistics is not just a flow of boxes, but a flow of hope. Moreover, as the SCOR model posits for social development, total visibility of the supply chain in fluvial municipalities is the prerequisite for equity. The platform resolves the dichotomy between the capital and the periphery. It ensures that staff in Solano and Getucha have the same decision-making tools as those in the central level in Florencia, thereby eliminating the "geography of oblivion" (HIMSS, 2024).

From the perspective of Social Justice, the reduction in out-of-pocket expenditure documented in our findings demonstrates that digital transformation is essentially a poverty alleviation policy. Furthermore, integrating Pharmacovigilance and Molecular Traceability in areas where connectivity is a luxury positions ESEFJL as a national benchmark in bridge-building. Consequently, the system not only delivers the correct drug; it ensures that the State is present in every dispensing event, guaranteeing that the right to health does not depend on the user's zip code (OPS, 2023).

Model sustainability remains a key focus. The institutional resilience demonstrated in the face of local challenges suggests that the ESEFJL model can be replicated in other regions with similar geographic barriers. The transparency of our data has created a new level of trust between the institution and our patients. The motto "Revive Health, Fighting from the Heart!" is now a measurable reality rather than just a corporate goal.

11. General Recommendations

"Clinical excellence begins with administrative discipline in the pharmaceutical service."

A. Strategic Governance Axis

  • Model Institutionalization: Establish the ESEFJL Pharmacy ecosystem as the mandatory operating standard for all future units, ensuring network scalability.
  • Open Data Policy: Implement public dashboards for citizen oversight of drug availability, strengthening institutional transparency.

B. Technical-Operational Axis

  • Infrastructure Maintenance: Ensure contingency plans for satellite connectivity in fluvial municipalities to avoid data synchronization gaps.
  • Reorder Automation: Progressively migrate toward predictive analysis models that anticipate seasonal demand peaks (regional epidemiology).

C. Clinical Axis and Patient Safety

  • Zero Error Tolerance: Enforce tripartite validation for every dispensing event before final delivery, using system-generated QR codes.
  • Pharmacovigilance Culture: Encourage non-punitive reporting of logistical incidents to feed the continuous improvement cycle.
