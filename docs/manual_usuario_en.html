1. Institutional Philosophy and Technological Purpose

The ESEFJL Pharmacy platform was designed to be the primary support for clinical safety at ESE Fabio Jaramillo Londoño. In our Amazonian context, managing medical supplies presents unique logistical challenges. This manual helps standardize operations as a public health mission.

Safety Mission:

Our goal is Zero Tolerance for medication errors. Every entry in the system must be an exact reflection of the physical warehouse to guarantee safe and timely treatments.

Operator Ethical Protocol

Pharmacy staff are the final safety filter before medication reaches the citizen. Correct use of ESEFJL Pharmacy ensures:

  • Transparency: Every movement is recorded with the user's audit stamp.
  • Efficiency: Waste reduction allows for greater investment in preventive health.
  • Humanization: Expediting delivery reduces waiting times for vulnerable patients.

2. Comprehensive System Operation

Navigation in ESEFJL Pharmacy has been designed to be intuitive yet rigorous. The standard workflow is divided into three critical phases:

Phase I: Authentication and Context Loading

Upon logging in, the system automatically identifies your **Operational Branch**. This is vital, since the stock you view corresponds only to your current physical location. If you need to manage another branch, you must have Manager-level administrative permissions.

Phase II: Dashboard (Home Central)

The "Home Central" is your command center. Here you must monitor the **Expiration Traffic Lights** and **Reorder Points**. An orange alert means you must initiate the request process to the Central Warehouse in Florencia immediately.

3. Pharmacovigilance Protocols and Weighting Alerte

The ESE adopts international expiration control standards through dynamic visual alerts. ESEFJL Pharmacy automates this analysis every 24 hours.

🛑 RED ALERT (Critical)

Range: < 90 days to expiration.

Action: Segregate in the "Quarantine Area" and report for possible redistribution or immediate exit plan.

⚠️ ORANGE ALERT (Attention)

Range: 90 - 180 days to expiration.

Action: Strictly apply FEFO protocol (First Expired, First Out) on the front shelf.

✅ GREEN ALERT (Safe)

Range: > 180 days.

Action: Standard storage according to humidity and temperature conditions recommended by the laboratory.

4. Citizen Service and Enrollment (SIAU)

Patient enrollment is the first link in the health chain. An incorrect record in the SIAU (Information and Citizen Service) can lead to severe administrative penalties.

Scheme and Billing Validation

When registering a patient, the system requires classifying the health scheme. This automatically determines if the medication belongs to the PBS (Health Benefits Plan) or requires shared payment (Non-PBS/Copayment).

  • Law 1448 of 2011: If the patient is marked as "Vulnerable Population/Displaced", the system will automatically exempt the payment of copayments according to national mandates.
  • Document Validation: The physical document must be verified before entering the number. Once registered, the patient's record becomes immutable to protect their pharmacy clinical history.

5. High-Precision Technical Dispatch Protocol

The delivery record is the most important transaction in the system. Follow the **"5 Rights"** method integrated into ESEFJL Pharmacy:

  1. Right Patient: Validation through identity card number in the dynamic search engine.
  2. Right Medication: Confirmation of the generic name and concentration (e.g., Acetaminophen 500mg).
  3. Right Quantity: Manual entry of units according to the physical medical prescription.
  4. Right Batch: The system automatically selects the batch with the closest expiration date (FEFO). Verify that it matches the physical box.
  5. Right Record: Upon clicking "Register Delivery", the transaction is irrevocable and the accounting discount is issued.
Technical Note: ESEFJL Pharmacy generates an automatic messaging log (SMS/Email) notifying the central system about the delivery, ensuring that stock replenishment is activated without manual intervention.

6. Auditing and Billing Grievances Management (Glosas)

A "Glosa" is an invoice return due to data inconsistencies. ESEFJL Pharmacy mitigates this risk through **Preventive Auditing**.

Inconsistency Action Protocol:

  • Stock Difference: If the system shows 0 but there is physical product, DO NOT perform the delivery. You must report to the Regent to adjust the batch entry.
  • Medicine Not Listed: Contact the Florencia Warehouse to enable the ATC code for the medicine in your municipal branch.
  • Typing Error: If you made a mistake in the quantity, inform the System Administrator in less than 1 hour to perform the corresponding technical cancellation.

7. Institutional Glossary of Terms

ATC (Anatomical Therapeutic Chemical):
Hierarchical classification of drugs according to the organ or system on which they act.
CEDIS (Distribution Center):
Central ESE warehouse in Florencia that supplies the entire departmental network.
FEFO (First Expired, First Out):
Rotation protocol where the product that expires first leaves first.
SIAU:
Information and Citizen Service, responsible for the initial patient enrollment.
UPC (Capitation Payment Unit):
Annual value recognized for each of the affiliates to the health system.
